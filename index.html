Why the Pomodoro Technique Works

The Pomodoro Technique breaks your work into focused 25-minute intervals separated by short breaks. Working in short, defined bursts makes big tasks feel approachable and gives your brain a clear finish line to aim for.

The regular breaks matter just as much as the focus. Stepping away for a few minutes lets your mind rest, reduces burnout, and keeps your concentration fresh across a long day. After four focus sessions, a longer break helps you fully recharge before the next round.

Over time, counting completed sessions turns productivity into something you can actually see — a simple, motivating measure of the focused work you put in each day.

Who We Are

Pomora is a calm, focused space for getting meaningful work done. We built it because we wanted a timer that felt less like a stopwatch and more like a quiet room — soft visuals, gentle sound, and just enough structure to keep you moving.

Our goal is simple: help you spend less time fighting distraction and more time in flow. Whether you're studying, writing, coding, or working through a long to-do list, Pomora is designed to fade into the background and let the work take center stage.

Built for Good

Pomora is more than a productivity tool. As we grow, a share of our revenue goes toward charitable causes — because we believe the time you invest in your work can also help make the world a little better.
